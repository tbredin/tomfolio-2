More about me — I'm a kiwi who came to Melbourne in 2014, currently residing in the lovely city of Merri-Bek. My background is in both design and development. I studied a hybrid Computer Graphic Design degree back in the mid 2000s. I've spoken at MelbCSS, A11yBytes Melbourne, DesignOps Melbourne, and A11yCamp Sydney, on topics such as web typography, accessibility testing approaches, and design systems collaboration. Back in 2016 I released the MegaType vertical rhythm system which made waves in the web typography community and promoted renewed discussion about support for typographic units on the web (such as cap-height or x-height), and promoting early discussion of other forward thinking web typography features, like leading-trim.

Design systems and reusable components have been a big career focus of mine. In my last role as Senior Design Systems Engineer I was leading a component library project with some unique challenges like code sharing across multiple websites and across some pockets of legacy code as well. I take a very tech-agnostic approach, with a belief that a different technology stack will suit the unique needs and skills of each team and project. For this component library I led a tech research sprint and open RFC process with the team, opting for web components using Lit 3 due to their impressive interoperability and performance characteristics.

I've led the tech on a lot of design systems projects over the years, including Department of Health and Human Services Intranet (internal system, a client we had at Today), WorkSafe Victoria's component library (client at Today), or Merri-Bek City Council's component library (Liquorice client).

Browser performance — I can assess rendering with confidence. One recent performance-focused project I led was the front end for the ACMI Story Of the Moving Image Online Exhibition while working at Liquorice — where we needed to optimise rendering of very very long web pages with a lot of media on them. We also had animations on these pages that needed to be well optimised and smooth at 60fps. Both of these challenges required attention given to browser rendering — profiling scroll perf, paint flashes and compositing to ensure the GPU is not doing unnecessary work and optimising where we could to reduce render load.

Accessibility excellence is also a core part of what I do, where I aim to comfortably surpass WCAG 2.2 AA with everything I do. Accessibility is a practice, which requires years of industry experience and a discerning eye to do it well — inclusive design truly cannot be reduced to a simple checklist. I combine automated and manual testing approaches which ensure we do not leave anyone behind.
